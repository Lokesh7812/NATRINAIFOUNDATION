 1. Tamil Nadu Hospital Approval Roadmap (Step-by-Step)

In Tamil Nadu, hospitals are regulated mainly under:

Tamil Nadu Clinical Establishments (Regulation) Act, 1997

TN Health & Family Welfare Department rules

Plus central rules like Biomedical Waste, Fire Safety, etc.

As per TN rules, every hospital must register and meet minimum standards for space, staffing, and equipment (Indian Kanoon)

PHASE 1 — Planning & Land Stage (Before construction)

 1. Land Use Conversion (Most critical first step)

Land must be Institutional / Hospital use

Apply for Land Use Conversion (CLU) from DTCP / CMDA / Local body

 2. Building Plan Approval

Submit hospital building drawings to:

Local Panchayat / Municipality / Corporation

CMDA / DTCP (depending on area)

 3. Fire Department “In-Principle” design clearance

Required for hospitals above certain beds (especially >10 beds)

PHASE 2 — Design Compliance Stage (VERY IMPORTANT)

Before final construction, design must comply with:

 Clinical Establishment Minimum Standards

Must follow TN rules for:

Minimum floor space

Staff requirements

Equipment norms (Indian Kanoon)

 Other mandatory compliance layers:

NBC (National Building Code)

Fire & Life Safety Code

Biomedical Waste Rules

Barrier-free access (PwD norms)

AERB (if X-ray / CT / radiation)

PHASE 3 — Construction Stage Inspections

Executive Summary

Genesis Soccer School is a flagship youth development initiative established by Natrinai to create Puducherry's first structured football development ecosystem connecting Government Schools, community football programs, talent identification initiatives, coach development programs, and elite athlete pathways.

The project seeks to address the lack of accessible football infrastructure, quality coaching, and athlete development opportunities available to children from economically disadvantaged communities. Through a comprehensive two-year intervention, Genesis Soccer School will establish football programs across 50 Government Schools, create a dedicated football training centre, develop a high-potential athlete pathway, and strengthen local coaching capacity.

The project will directly engage more than 2,500 students, support 50 elite football athletes, train 100 Physical Education Teachers and community coaches, and create sustainable football development opportunities that continue beyond the project period.

Genesis Soccer School is built upon years of grassroots football development work led by Mr. Mohan Dass, whose commitment to mentoring underprivileged children has demonstrated the transformative power of football as a tool for social change.

This initiative seeks to scale that proven model and establish a sustainable football ecosystem that empowers young people through sport, education, leadership, and opportunity.

Organizational Background

Natrinai is a community-driven social development organization committed to empowering children and young people through education, sports, leadership development, and community engagement.

Through initiatives including the MERO Trophy, grassroots football development programs, educational support initiatives, athlete assistance programs, and coach development workshops, Natrinai has worked to expand opportunities for underserved communities across Puducherry.

Our philosophy is simple:

"No talented child should be denied an opportunity because of financial circumstances."

This principle guides all our interventions and forms the foundation of Genesis Soccer School.

Problem Statement

Puducherry has a rich football culture and significant untapped sporting talent. However, children from low-income and rural communities continue to face major barriers that limit participation and development.

Key challenges include:

• Limited access to qualified football coaches.

• Lack of football infrastructure and training facilities.

• Financial barriers preventing participation.

• Inadequate access to sports equipment.

• Limited opportunities for competitive exposure.

• Absence of structured talent identification pathways.

• Lack of community-level football development systems.

As a result, many talented young athletes remain undiscovered and unsupported.

The absence of structured football development pathways not only limits sporting achievement but also reduces opportunities for youth engagement, leadership development, confidence building, and positive social participation.

Genesis Soccer School seeks to address these challenges through a sustainable and scalable football development model.

Project Goal

To establish a sustainable football development ecosystem across Puducherry that empowers children and young people through football, education, leadership development, and structured athlete pathways.

Project Objectives

Establish football development programs in 50 Government Schools.

Provide structured football coaching for more than 2,500 students.

Create a dedicated Genesis Soccer School Training Centre.

Identify and support 50 high-potential football athletes.

Strengthen coaching quality through capacity-building initiatives.

Promote leadership, discipline, teamwork, and life skills among youth.

Develop pathways connecting grassroots football to regional and national opportunities.

Rural development
Hut-to-Home Rural Housing & Poverty Alleviation Project

Project Background

Housing is one of the most fundamental human needs. Despite significant development across India, many families continue to live in temporary huts, thatched shelters, and structurally unsafe dwellings that expose them to rain, heat, flooding, health hazards, and social insecurity.

For families living below the poverty line, inadequate housing is not merely a shelter issue and it directly affects health, education, safety, dignity, and economic opportunities. Children struggle to study in overcrowded and unsafe environments, women face challenges related to privacy and sanitation, and elderly family members remain vulnerable to harsh weather conditions.

Recognizing this challenge, Natrinai Rural Development Initiative proposes the Kudisai Maatru Thittam (Hut-to-Home transformation Project), a community-driven housing intervention designed to transform the lives of vulnerable families by replacing unsafe huts with permanent, dignified homes constructed on land owned by the beneficiaries.

Proof of Concept

Before developing this larger initiative, our team successfully implemented a housing transformation project for a vulnerable family in Kulithalai, located in Karur District.

The completed house had a total construction value of ₹13.20 lakh.

The project was jointly supported by:

Mr. Tharun A.P.

Mr. Mohan Dass

Mr. Bala Krishnan

Together, the team contributed ₹10.20 lakh towards construction costs.

The remaining amount was supported by Siveon Groups, enabling successful completion of the project.

The project demonstrated that targeted housing support can significantly improve living conditions, dignity, and long-term stability for economically disadvantaged families.

Building upon this successful model, Natrinai now aims to expand the initiative to support multiple families through a structured housing development programme.

Project Vision

To ensure that every vulnerable family has access to safe, secure, and dignified housing, thereby reducing poverty and improving quality of life.

Project Mission

To identify deserving families living in unsafe housing conditions and provide permanent homes that promote health, safety, education, and social inclusion.

Proposed Project

Phase I – 25 Houses for 25 Families

The first phase of the project proposes the construction of 25 permanent houses for 25 vulnerable families across selected rural and semi-rural communities.

Each beneficiary family will own both the land and the completed house, creating a long-term asset that can be passed on to future generations.

1. Executive Summary

The Natrinai 52 Scholars Initiative is a structured, community-driven education empowerment program designed to support 52 economically disadvantaged students every year (one student per week) through financial assistance, academic mentoring, and career guidance.

The program is implemented by the Natrinai Foundation in partnership with the Dr. Abdul Kalam Dream Foundation (Academic Delivery Partner) and is supported by a trained internal Natrinai Teaching and Mentorship Team.

This initiative ensures that students from below-poverty and economically vulnerable backgrounds are not deprived of education due to financial hardship.
